Low agility/magic farming guide for Task Accounts
Overview

This guide is for task accounts that need early farming levels, especially levels 1-34, and have few available teleports. Once you hit 34 Farming, if you still need more levels, you’re allowed (and advised) to get 100% Hosidius favor and do the rest of your training at Tithe Farm.

General Strategy

The bulk of your experience will come from making supercompost by buying pineapples from charter ships and using the supercompost on your plants. The other xp will come from planting and harvesting crops.

*Note: This guide does not include farming hops during your farm runs. While doing early-level farming myself, I found I didn’t have enough time for them, so I’m skipping their inclusion.

Initial Preparation
  • Before starting your farm runs, you’ll want to do some preparation.
    • Buy a boatload of pineapples from charter ships or the Grand Tree food store. Catherby or Port Khazard charter stores are both good options because they’re close to a bank. 200-300 is a good starting amount.
    • Get some allotment and flower seeds (instructions below). A hundred or so are good to start with.
    • Buy 4 or 5 bucket packs from Sarah in the Falador farming store (in the house west of the farming patches) and deposit the buckets into a tool leprechaun at any farming spot. These are for holding supercompost.
    • Get some cash. Charters are not cheap, but they’re the best way to get around with no teles. Getting an inventory of steel plates in the wilderness with a looting bag is the most straightforward way to do this.
Obtaining Seeds
  1. Do you have 38 thieving?
    • Pickpocket Master Farmers
  2. Otherwise, buy seeds from Olivia in Draynor Village. The best type of allotment seed she stocks is cabbage, and the best flower seed she stocks is rosemary.
What to do at the farms

Time to actually gain xp. Do this by running to any number of allotment patches and doing the following actions:

  • Withdraw buckets from the leprechaun, grab the 15 supercompost you made last run, then deposit the super compost into the leprechaun.
  • Use 15 more pineapples on the compost bin to start making more super compost.
  • Harvest your completed crops, and use them on the leprechaun to turn them into notes and save inventory space.
  • Plant your allotment and flower seeds. Use your supercompost on them. Not only does this keep the plants alive, but you get xp for using the compost.
  • Head to the next patch!
Which patches should I use?

There are 4 patches you can consider using for early game training:

  • Falador farm
  • Catherby
  • Ardy farm
  • Hosidius farm

You don’t have to do all of them. Maybe you only have the time/run energy to do three of the patches. That’s fine. Or you might find it too annoying to run all the way to Hosidius. That’s cool too.

With zero teleports, one possible rotation can look like:
  1. Home teleport to Lumbridge
  2. Run to the Falador Farm.
  3. Farm in Falador.
  4. Run to Port Sarim, and take the charter to Catherby.
  5. Farm in Catherby.
  6. Take the charter back to Port Sarim
  7. Run west to the boat in Rimmington that takes you to the Ardougne/Brimhaven. Take it to Ardougne. Run to the Ardougne farming patch.
  8. Farm in Ardougne.
  9. Minigame tele to Pest Control, then take the boat back to Port Sarim
  10. Run to Veos in the northernmost boat section, and take the boat to Zeah.
  11. Run to the Hosidius farming patch.
  12. Farm in Hosidius.
  13. Repeat!

Once you get some teleports, this can be shortened dramatically. Falador and Camelot teleports really help out, as does the Xeric’s Talisman teleport. Also, once you go into Tithe Farm one time, you can start using the Tithe Farm minigame tele for future farm runs.

Tip: If you get a Garden Pie from Young or Gourmet implings or the Cooking Guild, you can boost to get into Tithe Farm at 31 Farming to unlock the teleport 3 levels early.

Ancient Page Guide
  1. Enter Ancient Cavern.
  2. Wait 10 minutes to lose aggro. Runelite's "NPC Aggression Timer" plugin can show you how long you need to wait. (You don't have to, but you risk getting slapped by a dragon on the way down)
  3. Step on the marked tile. That tile is inconveniently one step outside the aggro range.
  4. Run back up to the entrance immediately after. You can try to take the aggro of a waterfiend to save a dragon slap.
  5. Wait another 10 minutes.
  6. Go back on that tile.
  7. You can now loot the skeleton from that tile without getting attacked from spawning skeletons. Hop and repeat.
  8. ???
  9. Profit!
  10. PS: The rummage damage scales slightly with your current hitpoints. If you only eat when below 10 hp, you can't die and get more rummages per inventory of food.
